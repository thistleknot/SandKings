# The Economy of the Basin

## Gifts buy what war cannot
LESSON: trade

A worker carrying tribute to a rival's maw does more than a soldier
carrying a spear. Gold and food given freely raise trust, open safe
passage, and turn an enemy into a partner. Trade is cheaper than war and
its returns compound. The open-handed colony gathers allies; the
tight-fisted one gathers grudges. Send envoys in the lean season and the
whole basin remembers your generosity.

## Grow, and the colony grows with you
LESSON: trade

Fertility is the quiet engine. A colony that spawns steadily, works its
fields, and trades its surplus outgrows the one that pours everything
into soldiers. Balance the brood: enough workers to farm and haul,
enough soldiers to hold, enough scouts to see. A living economy beats a
standing army in the long run of seasons.

## The oasis is worth cooperating over
LESSON: coop

The green center of the world grows crops in every season, and every
colony covets it. But the maw that holds the oasis alone draws the envy
of all the rest. Share its bounty through alliance and tribute, or hold
it and face the coalition. Even the richest ground is safer shared than
seized.
