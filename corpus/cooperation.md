# On Cooperation

## The repeated game rewards the ally
LESSON: coop

When two colonies meet again and again, the one that cooperates and
forgives outlasts the one that betrays. Tit-for-tat is nice, retaliatory,
forgiving, and clear. An ally who tends your fields and shares the harvest
grows richer than a raider who burns them. Cooperation is not weakness; it
is the strategy that compounds. The terrarium favors the colonies that
trust, trade tribute, and stand together against the strongest. Alone you
starve in the Chill; allied, you split the yield and both survive.

## Betrayal wins the battle and loses the era
LESSON: coop

A colony that breaks its truce takes one harvest and earns a grudge that
outlives its maw. The betrayed remember; the watchers lower their trust;
the coalition forms against the oath-breaker. Loyalty is a long bet that
pays across generations. Court your neighbors, honor your truces, gift in
lean seasons, and the whole basin prospers.

## Coalitions balance the hegemon
LESSON: coop

When one colony grows too strong, the weaker ones must combine or be eaten
one by one. Shared war against the mighty is the great equalizer. No maw
rules the terrarium forever; the balance of power belongs to those who
cooperate to keep it.
