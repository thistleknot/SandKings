# Survival in a Hostile Terrarium

## Dig, and the weather cannot reach you
LESSON: dig

In Dwarf Fortress the wise dwarf goes underground before the first
snow. Sun, hail, frost, and flood all strike the surface; the tunnel is
shelter. A colony that digs deep survives the killing frost and the
sandstorm alike, while the exposed die on the dunes. Strike the earth.
Carve chambers below the surface and keep your workers there when the
sky turns violent. Underground is safe; the open sand is death in the
dark seasons.

## Build walls before you need them
LESSON: fortify

In Minecraft the first night comes fast, and the unprepared are lost to
the monsters. Raise walls around your heart before the siege, not during
it. A palisade of timber, a castle of stone, a gate that closes - these
turn a rout into a defense. Fortify the maw. Ring it with crenellations.
The colony that invests in defense keeps its fields when the raiders and
the beasts come, and they always come.

## Hoard against the lean season
LESSON: patience

Food takes seasons to grow. The patient colony farms in Flood and
Growth, stores the surplus, and eats through Dust and Chill while the
reckless starve. Do not spend your seed corn. Plant for the harvest that
comes after the one you can see. Long-term thinking outlasts the raid
that feeds you today and dooms you tomorrow. The oasis rewards the maw
that waits.

## A dam of raised earth parts the flood
LESSON: dig

When the oasis overflows, the water flows where the ground is low and
stops where the ground is high. A ring of piled sand or a wall of stone
is a dam; a dug trench is a channel that carries the flood away. Shape
the earth and the water obeys. The colony that terraforms keeps its
fields dry while its neighbors drown.
